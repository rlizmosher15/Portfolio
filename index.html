Digital Sketchbook

Take a look at my Workflow!

Preliminary Sketches

This is the first stage of any project that I work on. This is where ideas are scribbled, and compositions are tested. 

Value and Lighting Studies

Once a sketch has been chosen to move forward with, I then do a variety of value lighting scenarios to build the proper atmosphere. 

Lighting and Color Studies

When the light source has been determined and the values have been refined, I create color compositions to refine the mood of the piece. 

Final Illustration

Finally, it's all about refining and rendering. This is where I focus on details, textures, and bring the piece together. 

About Me... and Jack 

Here you will see me with my partner in crime Jack, the goodest boy around. 

I began my art journey as a simple hobby in highschool. I attended the Rochester Institute of Technology for 3D Digital Design. I then transferred to the Academy of Art University online program for visual Development (cause ya know, Covid...). Here I got my AA degree and found a particular love for illustration. 

I incorporate eyes as the primary subject in a lot of my work. Mental Illness is a common theme as well. 

Frequently Asked Questions

Do you have any formal art training, or are you self-taught?

Both! I attended the Rochester Institute of Technology and studied 3D Digital Design for a year and a half. I then transferred to the Academy of Art University in San Fransisco, CA where I obtained an Associates of Art in Visual Development. Many of my skills however were learned through self driven projects and deliberate studies. 

What equipment and software do you use to create your work?

I work primarily in Procreate on my IPad, however I sometimes switch to Adobe Photoshop and my Wacom Cintiq for larger projects.
